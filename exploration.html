Interesting columns:¶

In a first inspection of the dataframe the shape revealed 636 columns. To reduce the complexity in the analysis and speed up the analysis I identified key columns on which I will focus. I mainly focused on the maths grades as well as how homework and the parents influence the scores.

  • STIDSTD: Student ID
  • ST01Q01: International Grade
  • ST03Q01: Birth - Month
  • PARED: Highest parental education in years
  • OUTHOURS: Out-of-School Study Time

  • FISCED: Educational level of father (ISCED)

Structure of data:¶

The table has data entries for 485490 students of 66 countries. It has numerical values for example for the test scores as well as ordinal categorical values like the attitude towards school.

Main dataset features:¶

The main dataset features are the grades which are the outcomes of most or all other columns.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

Because the original dataset has over 600 columns, I reduced it to the columns with general inforamtion like age and country aswell as more specific columns related to the parents and situation at home of the student.

Talk about some of the relationships you observed in this part of the investigation. Were there features that strengthened each other in terms of looking at your feature(s) of interest?¶

A relathionship between the attitude of the student and his grades is visible. If the student has a positive attitude toward it like enjoying good grades the test scores are higher the more he agrees. However when the agreement is strong with negative attitudes like school is a waste of time, the average grades are worse the stronger he agrees.

Were there any interesting or surprising interactions between features?¶

The heatmap of test scores, minuits studied and education level of the parent has a couple interesting insights. First, the students with the highest scores are all within the first third of study time. Second when students study a lot the minimum scores rise so studying a lot more does help to increase the score. However the max scores decreases the more student studies which suggests that there is a limit in terms of score a student can achieve no matter how much he studies. Third the higher the education level of the parents the more students are present studying a lot (more than 600 min per week).
